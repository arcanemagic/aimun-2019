The First Conference

All prepped and propped, you’d see all the delegates walking towards their respective committees with their polished shoes clipping-clopping, one hand swaying in the air whilst brushing their hair and the other clenching a laptop or files under their arms. But what you can’t see are the dark circles beneath their eyes gifted by the act of sitting in front of their computers for hours and researching exhaustively, and some young anxious delegates who’d be clenching their hands into a fist and nibbling their nails on the way.

From the perspective of a first-timer, I’d say that the first day usually goes into adjusting and acclimatising to the extremely formal atmosphere that MUNs provide. The delegate might be as well prepared as the others but will still find him/herself clueless of what to do and what not to. Keeping aside the exceptional ones who are very confident of themselves from the first day only, what do you expect a first timer to do in an unmoderated caucus? If you think that the delegate would either sit behind the laptop pretending to mind his/her own business or approach another delegate, hesitantly to be included in one of the conversations, then you’re not mistaken. You could always find some delegates in every committee who’d be oblivious of the committee proceedings. However, debates like moderated caucuses and other discussions involving speaking from their own seating, are comfort zones for such people as they don’t call for a one-on-one debate that some might find intimidating. Gradually after being able to comprehend the working of this system, their hesitation starts to fade away, like mist when wind blows and by the end of the conference they come out as confident experienced delegates.

Your first experience might not be a deciding factor but it sure gives you a much-needed experience and confidence to face upcoming challenges.

Tanishi Adhikari

Class XI, Amity International School, Saket
